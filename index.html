About Me

I am a Post-doctoral Researcher specializing in the evolution of corporate and managerial communication. By applying Natural Language Processing (NLP) and computational analysis, I investigate how the "performativity" of language influences monetary policy and industrial digitization in Europe.

Currently a Research Fellow at Luiss Business School, I hold a PhD from Ca’ Foscari University of Venice. My background bridges the gap between deep Linguistics and modern Data Science.

Research Portfolio

Performative Language of the ECB

Analyzing the "doing by saying" effect in monetary authority. I focus on executive utterances from 1999–2021 to understand the empowerment of the ECB through language.

Global Network of Central Bankers

Extraction of mentioned entities in communication (2007–2021) to map the interaction patterns and co-influence within global financial leadership.

Building Blocks of Communication

Applying Topic Modeling (LDA) to a vast corpus of annual reports (1999–2020) to track the evolution of priorities across European central banks.

Mobile Banking Customer Experience

Sentiment analysis and text mining of customer reviews (2010–2022) to identify critical appreciation factors for European banking apps.

ECB vs. National Banks Distance

Measuring the communicative "proximity" between the supranational authority (ECB) and national central banks within the common monetary framework.
