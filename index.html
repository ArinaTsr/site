Customer experience with mobile banking apps (2010–2022)

European significant banks — Google play reviews analysis

How do customers experience mobile banking apps, and what aspects of app performance drive satisfaction or frustration? Traditional surveys capture limited feedback, but unstructured customer reviews offer rich insights into users’ priorities.

Data & Tools

  • Data: 50,000+ customer reviews scraped from Google Play Store for banks under direct ECB supervision.
  • Libraries: google-play-scraper, langdetect, spaCy, NLTK.
  • Visualization: Tableau

Method

  1. Scraping & Language Detection: Collected reviews per bank app. Due to the unavailability of spaCy pipelines for several languages, I excluded some banks. For Greek banks, Greek and English were both used.
  2. Preprocessing: Cleaned text, removed stopwords + generic words ("bank", "application").
  3. NLP Analysis:
    • Used spaCy pipelines for nouns (issues/features) and adjectives (sentiment/attitudes).
    • Counted most frequent 10 nouns + 10 adjectives per star rating (1–5).
  4. Visualization: Built an interactive Tableau dashboard showing sentiment evolution and top criticized aspects.

Findings

  • Praised: update, ease in making payments, ease of use, intuitive design.
  • Criticized: bugs in updates, security, connection, login issues, speed, lack of personalization.

Performative language of the ECB (1999–2021)

Analyzing modality in central bank communication

Central bank communication is not only descriptive — it can actively shape market expectations and political integration. This project investigates how the performative dimension has evolved across two decades.

The European Central Bank (ECB) was originally designed as a technocratic institution. Members of the Executive Board define the ECB’s public voice, constructing the institution’s identity through speeches. I examine how that identity has evolved by focusing on modality.

Data & Tools

  • Data: ECB Speeches Dataset (1999–2021).
  • Libraries: NLTK, spaCy.

Method

  1. Preprocessing: Tokenization and lemmatization across multiple languages.
  2. Modality Parsing: Identified epistemic modality (beliefs, probabilities) and deontic modality (commitments, obligations).
  3. Temporal Analysis: Tracked shifts in modality use over 22 years.

Findings (work in progress)

  • Crisis periods (2008–2012): Higher frequency of deontic modality signaling a strong intervention stance.
  • The ECB’s communication style has contributed to its institutional empowerment.

Global network of central banks and central bankers (2007–2021)

Entity recognition and network analysis of BIS speech corpus

Central banks are not isolated actors. This project explores how they reference each other and external organizations, revealing patterns of co-influence.

Data & Tools

  • Data: Central bank speeches from the BIS, scraped via Selenium.
  • Libraries: NetworkX, spaCy (NER), Pyvis.

Method

  1. Named-Entity Recognition (NER): Extracted organizations and individuals.
  2. Normalization: Merged coreferences using ECB abbreviations glossary.
  3. Community Detection: Applied Louvain method to identify clusters of institutions and policymakers.

Distance between the ECB and national central banks (1999–2020)

Measuring convergence in eurosystem communication

How closely aligned are the communication strategies of the ECB and NCBs? This project utilizes text-based distance metrics.

Data & Tools

  • Data: Introductions to annual reports of 11 NCBs.
  • Methods: Topic modeling (25 pre-defined topics), Hellinger distance.

Method

  1. Topic Modeling: Represented each text as a distribution of topics.
  2. Distance Calculation: Measured similarity using Hellinger distance.
  3. Visualization: Built a Tableau dashboard.

Building blocks of central banks’ communication (1999–2020)

Topic modeling of eurosystem annual reports

Central banks shift their focus in response to global and national shocks. This project investigates themes over two decades.

Data & Tools

  • Data: Annual reports of 11 NCBs and the ECB.
  • Libraries: Gensim (LDA), spaCy.
  • Visualization: Tableau dashboard.

Findings

  • Crisis years (2008–2012): Sharp rise in attention to financial stability and sovereign debt.
  • ECB vs NCBs: Trends converged, but national banks highlighted local issues like employment or fiscal policy.
