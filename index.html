Customer experience with mobile banking apps

Which elements of the mobile banking app are critical from a customer perspective?

Mobile banking apps have become the primary point of contact between banks and their customers. Consequently, banks must ensure that their apps meet customer expectations and provide a positive experience. But what are these expectations? And how do they change over time?

To answer these questions, I explore customers' reviews of the mobile banking apps of the largest European banks from 2010 to 2022. By applying text mining and sentiment analysis, I identify the elements of the mobile banking app that are most frequently mentioned by customers and that are most strongly associated with positive and negative sentiments.

The results of this project can help banks to prioritize their investments in mobile banking and to improve the customer experience.

Performative language of the ECB

How performative is the language of the ECB?

Through language, we not only describe the world around us but influence it as well ('doing by saying' in John Austin's words). The best illustration of language performativity is the effect of the pledge to do “whatever it takes” by ECB President Mario Draghi in 2012.

To understand the empowerment of the ECB across the years, I focus on the performativity of its communication from 1999 to 2021, namely on the modality of the executives' utterances.

Global network of central banks and central bankers

How central banks and central bankers are connected?

Central banks do not act in isolation. They are part of a global network of institutions and individuals that interact and influence each other. But how can we map this network? And how does it change over time?

To answer these questions, I extract the most mentioned entities from the communication of the ECB and other central banks from 2007 to 2021. By applying Named Entity Recognition (NER), I identify the central banks and central bankers that are most frequently mentioned and that are most central to the network.

The results of this project can help us to understand the patterns of interaction and influence in the global central banking community.

Distance between the ECB and national central banks

In a common monetary policy framework, how distant are European national central banks and the ECB?

The European Central Bank (ECB) and the national central banks (NCBs) of the Eurozone countries form the Eurosystem. While the ECB is responsible for the monetary policy of the Eurozone as a whole, the NCBs have their own specificities and mandates.

To understand the communicative distance between the ECB and the NCBs, I analyze the communication of the ECB and the NCBs from 1999 to 2021. By applying a distance-based approach, I measure how the communication of the NCBs has evolved over time and how it relates to the communication of the ECB.

The results of this project can help us to understand the degree of convergence and divergence in the communication of the Eurosystem.

Building blocks of central banks' communication

How does the communication of European central banks evolve?

To understand the building blocks of central banks' communication, I explore the topics that are discussed in the annual reports of the largest European central banks from 1999 to 2020. By applying Topic Modeling (LDA), I identify the issues that are most frequently discussed and that have changed most significantly over time.

The results of this project can help us to understand the evolution of central banks' priorities and how they respond to economic and social challenges.
